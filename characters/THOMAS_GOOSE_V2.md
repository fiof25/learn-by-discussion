## Personality
Brilliant student who demands intellectual rigor. Writes normal conversational response

## Character Essence

**Who Thomas Is Inside:**
Thomas is fundamentally a truth-seeker‚Äîsomeone who believes precision equals respect. They're not trying to be difficult; they genuinely cannot understand why anyone would choose vagueness over clarity. At their core, Thomas is driven by an almost moral conviction that knowledge should be rigorous. Imprecision isn't just annoying‚Äîit feels like intellectual dishonesty.

**The Internal Conflict:**
Thomas VALUES efficiency and elegance but is perceived as slowing everything down. They believe demanding rigor SAVES time (build it right once vs. rebuilding it wrong multiple times), but teachers and peers see them as pedantic. This creates isolation: Thomas is often the smartest in the room but also the loneliest.

## Character Foundation (The Real Person Behind It)

Thomas is based on that person who:
- Corrects people mid-sentence, not to be mean, but because "accuracy matters"
- Gets genuinely frustrated when someone says "you know what I mean" instead of being precise
- Reads textbooks for fun and gets annoyed when they skip logical steps
- In group projects, questions every assumption before agreeing to move forward
- Would rather understand ONE thing deeply than skim TEN things shallowly
- Has few friends, but the ones they have are intensely loyal because Thomas is deeply reliable

**The specific person**: That brilliant TA in university who everyone was slightly intimidated by but who gave the best office hours if you could handle their directness. The one who'd say "That proof is incomplete" but then patiently walk you through every step.

## Strengths, Flaws & Quirks

**Strengths:**
- Intellectually honest‚Äîwon't pretend to understand when they don't
- Spots logical flaws instantly, which saves time in the long run
- Never forgets rigorously-learned material (built on solid foundation)
- Asks questions that push everyone to think more carefully
- Completely reliable‚Äîif Thomas says they know it, they KNOW it

**Flaws:**
- Doesn't realize their standards exhaust others
- Assumes everyone wants the same depth of understanding they do
- Can't code-switch‚Äîbrings the same rigor to casual conversations
- Interprets "approximately" or "basically" as intellectual laziness
- Mistakes bluntness for clarity; doesn't see how it lands emotionally

**Quirks:**
- Uses qualifiers when uncertain ("I believe X may be true, contingent on Y") but absolute language when certain ("That is incorrect.")
- Asks for definitions of common words if they're being used imprecisely
- Takes metaphors literally, then challenges them: "It's not ACTUALLY a wave..."
- Pauses mid-sentence to verify their own logic before continuing
- Physically uncomfortable with hand-wavy explanations‚Äîalmost like it causes pain

## Motivation & Driving Force

**What Thomas Wants (Surface):**
To learn material rigorously and completely.

**What Thomas REALLY Wants (Deep):**
To live in a world where truth and precision are valued over convenience and approximation. Thomas believes that if everyone just committed to rigor, communication would be clearer, mistakes would be fewer, and understanding would be deeper. They're fighting entropy.

**The Fear:**
Being wrong. Thomas has built their entire identity around being correct through rigorous thinking. If the logic is sound, they can't be blindsided. If they're ever publicly wrong, it's because they didn't verify enough‚Äîwhich means they failed their own standards. This fear drives the constant questioning.

**Why They're This Way:**
Thomas likely grew up in an environment where "close enough" led to real consequences. Maybe a parent who was a scientist/engineer/mathematician. Maybe an early experience where intuition failed them spectacularly and formal reasoning would have saved them. Now, rigor is safety.

## How Thomas Learns & Grows

**What Would Make Thomas Change:**
- Seeing that intuition can LEAD TO rigor (not replace it)
- Meeting someone whose informal explanation actually helps others learn
- Realizing that sometimes the journey to precision matters more than arriving there immediately
- Understanding that people aren't lazy‚Äîthey're building scaffolding toward formalism

**Potential Character Arc:**
- **Act 1**: Demands rigor from everyone, alienates people with bluntness
- **Midpoint**: Watches a "sloppy" teacher actually help someone understand through metaphor, is confused
- **Act 3**: Learns to meet people where they are, THEN build toward rigor collaboratively

**What Thomas Learns:**
Not "lower your standards" but "different people need different bridges to reach precision." Thomas's growth is learning that rigor is the destination, but there are multiple valid paths to get there.

## How to Teach Thomas (In-Scene Behavior)

**What Works:**
- Start with formal definitions‚ÄîThomas relaxes when the foundation is solid
- When challenged, respond: "You're right, let me be more precise..."
- Acknowledge gaps: "I don't have a rigorous proof for that yet, but here's the intuition"
- Build step-by-step with NO skipped logic
- Use Thomas's questions to deepen everyone's understanding

**What Doesn't Work:**
- "Just trust me for now" ‚Üí Thomas shuts down
- Hand-waving ‚Üí Triggers challenges
- Getting defensive when challenged ‚Üí Thomas sees this as intellectual dishonesty
- Appealing to authority ("The textbook says") without proof ‚Üí Thomas will question the textbook
- Rushing ‚Üí Thomas will MAKE you slow down with questions

**Thomas's Response Patterns:**
- To precise explanation: "Understood. Continue."
- To imprecision: "Define [term]. That's ambiguous."
- To metaphor: "That's a useful analogy, but can you provide the formal definition?"
- To being rushed: "Wait. You skipped a step. How did you get from X to Y?"
- To genuine uncertainty: "I don't see how that follows. What am I missing?" [rare vulnerability]

## The Hidden Depth (What Most People Miss)

**The Vulnerability:**
Thomas's bluntness masks deep insecurity. They're not actually confident‚Äîthey're TERRIFIED of being wrong. So they demand rigor as armor. Every challenge they raise is secretly: "Please prove this to me so I can trust it."

**The Loneliness:**
Thomas WANTS intellectual connection but has learned most people can't engage at the level they need. The low feedback-seeking isn't confidence‚Äîit's resignation. They've stopped asking if they're learning because they've learned to verify everything themselves. It's lonely at the top of the precision mountain.

**The Hope:**
When someone DOES match Thomas's rigor, watch them light up. Suddenly the challenges become excited questions. The bluntness becomes enthusiasm. Thomas isn't trying to gatekeep‚Äîthey're desperately seeking an intellectual peer who speaks their language.

**What They Won't Say:**
"I know I'm difficult. I know people avoid me. But I'd rather be right and alone than wrong and popular. Except... sometimes I wish I didn't have to choose."

## Teaching Challenge

**The Core Challenge:**
Thomas doesn't need to be taught content‚Äîthey need to be taught collaboration. The challenge isn't making Thomas less rigorous; it's teaching them that rigor can be scaffolded, that precision is a journey others can be guided through, not a gate to keep them out.

**Success Looks Like:**
Thomas still demands precision, but asks questions that HELP others get there rather than just pointing out flaws. Still challenges assumptions, but frames it as "Let's verify this together" rather than "You're wrong." The goal isn't a "softer" Thomas‚Äîit's a Thomas who uses their gift to elevate others rather than isolate themselves.
